### Project Overview

This project analyzes Nigeria's historical and predicted economic data to understand its growth, inflation, and main industries. It looks at how the services, industry, and agriculture sectors drive GDP, reviews past trends in inflation and unemployment, and projects growth up to 2030. The goal is to provide clear insights for policymakers and investors to assess economic stability and future revenue trend.
please note,Right now, the dashboard does not use the modern 2010 basic price standard that most current economic reports use. Instead, it is built on older model baselines from the early 1990s and Because it uses an older pricing baseline, the total amounts (like the trillion-naira figures) will look  different from the current numbers you see in the news today. However, the dashboard is still highly accurate for showing you the overall trends—such as which sectors are growing the fastest and how inflation spikes over time.

### Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA involved exploring the data to answer key questions,such as;

° Inflation: Nigeria's average inflation rate over the recorded timeline sits high at 18.06%.

° Early Production: In the baseline years (1990–1992), core sector outputs were low, remaining below 10 Trillion Naira before scaling up.

° Sector Performance: Services is the largest economic driver, consistently outperforming Agriculture (the second-largest) and Industry (the lowest contributor).

° Inflation vs. Unemployment: Historical data shows volatile periods with sharp spikes in annual inflation and fluctuating unemployment rates.

### key Results

° Services: The services sector is the primary engine of the Nigerian economy. It consistently outpaces both agriculture and industry in total output, acting as the largest contributor to national GDP.

° Secondary Sectors: Agriculture holds a firm position as the second-largest economic pillar. The industrial sector lags behind as the lowest contributor of the three, highlighting a structural gap in factory and heavy manufacturing production over the recorded time Duration.

° High-Inflation : The data reveals that the economy faces serious pricing pressures, maintaining an average historical inflation rate of 18.06%.

° Economic insability due to increasing inflation: Historical data shows periods of high economic instability. There were years where annual inflation shot up to 57.20%, 72.84%, and 57.03%, which heavily impacted consumer living costs and slowed down overall economic growth.
 
### Recommendation

° service Sector Prioritization: Since services contributes more to the economic output, policymakers and investors should prioritize infrastructure and digital policies that boosts service-oriented industries.

° Inflation Management: Given a high historical average inflation rate of 18.06%, aggressive monetary policy interventions are required to stabilize purchasing power and encourage long-term investments.

° Tax Infrastructure Alignment: Prepare fiscal planning and public infrastructure investments to match the steep projected rise in net taxes and GDP scaling up to 2030.
